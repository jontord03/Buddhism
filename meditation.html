BUDDHIST MEDITATION

Overview

Meditation, in it of itself, is not exclusively Buddhist. However, there are many different Buddhist ways to practice meditation, such as Shamatha (Calm Abiding) meditation or Vipashyana (Insight) meditation.

Shamatha (Calm Abiding)

This is the form of meditation that most non-Buddhist's imagine when they think of Buddhist practice. When one practices Shamatha meditation, they seek to calm and quiet their mind. This can take the form of focusing on specific objects such as: love for hateful people, colors, causation for pride, or one's breath. Additionally, one can manifest the Four Immeasurables or the Four Abodes of Brahma such as: lovingkindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity.

Vipashyana (Insight)

The goal of Vipashyana is to realize no self, the idea that all people have great difficulty realizing. According to Buddha, it is not a question of whether or no the self exists, but rather the fact that our idea of self is empty and groundless. The three main objects of concentration are the 12 Links of Dependent Arising, the 5 Skandas, and Mindfulness. These are three advanced philosophical concepts that all address the fact that our reality is a intricately constructed dream from which we are too stubborn to wake.
